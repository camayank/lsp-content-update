## What "Weak Blog" Means Here

For this audit, a weak blog is any post that is missing most of the following:

- decision-stage intent in the title
- explicit problem framing
- direct answer in the opening
- comparison table, checklist, or due-date table
- penalty/risk/consequence section
- FAQ block
- strong service CTA
- modern SEO fields such as focus keyword and Rank Math scoring

The current archive is dominated by broad educational posts and thin explainers. That is useful for topical coverage, but not strong enough for AEO, GEO, AI retrieval, or service conversion.

## Main Finding

The content base is large, but most of it is generic, older, and not built around narrow founder intent.

That matches the opportunity you described:

- stop prioritizing generic education pages
- prioritize pages around comparison, missed compliance, penalty, applicability, and recovery intent
- convert every update into an answer-engine-friendly service entry point

## Highest-Priority Sitewide Shift

Move the blog from:

- "what is X"
- "overview of X"
- "simple guide to X"
- "benefits of X"

Toward:

- "X vs Y"
- "missed X filing: penalty and next steps"
- "is X applicable to me?"
- "documents required for X"
- "due date, late fee, and consequences of X"

Recommended treatment:

- make each surviving page target one exact intent, one CTA, and one entity set

## Standard Page Template for AEO + GEO + SEO

Every upgraded page should follow this structure:

1. Direct answer in the first 60 to 80 words
2. "Who is this for?" applicability section
3. decision table, due-date table, or checklist
4. risk / penalty / consequence block
5. documents required
6. step-by-step process or next steps
7. FAQs written in natural question language
8. one intent-matched CTA only

## AEO / GEO / AI Search Requirements

Every page should be optimized for retrieval and citation, not just ranking.

Required elements:

- answer-first intro paragraph
- scannable H2s with exact questions
- tables and checklists
- plain-English definitions for founders
- concise penalty and consequence summaries
- internal links to adjacent actions
- trust markers such as author, last updated month, and service capability
- FAQ schema and article schema
- one page = one core intent

Recommended retrieval-style headings:

- What is the due date for DIR-3 KYC?
- What happens if DIR-3 KYC is not filed on time?
- Who needs to file DPT-3?
- Is GST registration mandatory for home bakers?
- Which is better for a startup: LLP or Private Limited?

## CTA Logic to Use Everywhere

Match CTA to search intent:

- comparison page: `Book a structure-selection call`
- penalty page: `Get urgent compliance support`
- checklist/applicability page: `Get checklist + expert review`

Avoid generic homepage-style CTAs on these pages.
