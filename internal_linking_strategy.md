# Internal Linking Strategy for Rewrite Queue

## Purpose

Internal linking should improve:

- reader retention
- topic depth
- crawlability
- conversion flow
- AI retrieval context across related pages

## New queue fields

Added to all rewrite queue CSV files:

- `PRIMARY_INTERNAL_LINKS`
- `SUPPORTING_INTERNAL_LINKS`
- `CTA_LINK_TARGET`

## How to use them

### `PRIMARY_INTERNAL_LINKS`

These are the highest-priority contextual links to place inside the body of the article.

Use them:

- in the intro or summary block
- after the first or second main section
- inside the “next step” or “related compliance issue” area

Recommended count:

- 2 to 3 links from this field in a 2,500+ word article

### `SUPPORTING_INTERNAL_LINKS`

These are secondary links used to deepen the cluster.

Use them:

- inside FAQs
- inside side-note paragraphs
- in the conclusion
- in “also read” or “related issue” sections

Recommended count:

- 2 to 4 links from this field in a 2,500+ word article

### `CTA_LINK_TARGET`

This is the conversion destination or CTA theme the article should support.

Use it:

- in the final CTA section
- in a mid-article action box when urgency is high

## Recommended per-article linking pattern

For each rewrite:

1. Link to one comparison page
2. Link to one checklist or applicability page
3. Link to one penalty or recovery page
4. Link to one service or consultation page

## Anchor text guidance

Use descriptive anchors such as:

- `LLP annual compliance checklist`
- `DIR-3 KYC late fee and reactivation`
- `private company compliance checklist`
- `GST registration documents checklist`

Avoid:

- `click here`
- `read more`
- repeating the exact same anchor across every article

## Placement guidance

Best internal-link positions:

- after answering the core question
- where the reader naturally asks the next question
- near risk / penalty sections
- near checklist / document sections
- near the final CTA

## Cluster logic

### Comparison pages

Should link to:

- structure-registration service page
- compliance checklist
- penalty page

### Penalty or due-date pages

Should link to:

- recovery or filing support service page
- checklist page
- related foundational page

### Checklist or applicability pages

Should link to:

- due-date page
- penalty page
- service page

### Local service pages

Should link to:

- eligibility page
- documents page
- FAQ or checklist page

## Editorial rule

Do not add random links.

Every internal link should answer one of these:

- What should the reader do next?
- What related risk should the reader understand?
- Which adjacent page helps them make a decision?
- Which service page converts this intent into action?
