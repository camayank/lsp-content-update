## Task

Rewrite the source article into a fresh, fully unique, long-form Legal Suvidha blog post.

The final article must be:

- at least 2,500 words
- plain text only
- engaging and practical
- AEO optimized
- SEO optimized
- India-first or geo-appropriate
- AI-search-visibility ready
- suitable for direct use in the `Content` column of the developer import file

## Output Rules

Output plain text only.

Do not output HTML.

Do not include markdown code fences.

Use clear heading labels:

```text
H1:
H2:
H3:
```

Do not include commentary, analysis, or process notes.

## Required Article Structure

Use this structure unless the topic clearly requires a better order:

H1: Improved search-intent title

Opening direct answer in the first 80 words

Answer Box:

H2: What this topic means

H2: Why this matters in 2026

H2: Who this applies to

H2: Applicability, eligibility, or checklist

H2: Step-by-step process

H2: Documents or details required

H2: Risk, penalty, or consequences

H2: Common mistakes to avoid

H2: India-wide relevance or local relevance

H2: Practical next steps

H2: Frequently Asked Questions

H2: Conclusion

SEO_TITLE:

META_DESCRIPTION:

INTERNAL_LINK_ANCHORS:

## Content Requirements

1. Write at least 2,500 words.

2. Do not copy phrases verbatim from `ORIGINAL_CONTENT`.

3. Improve the article title for search intent. Do not blindly reuse the old title.

4. Start with a direct answer in the first 80 words.

5. Include an `Answer Box:` near the top.

6. Use short answer labels where useful:

- `Answer:`
- `Quick takeaway:`
- `Practical takeaway:`

7. Include checklists, numbered steps, or plain-text decision tables where useful.

8. Include a risk, penalty, consequence, or delay-cost section where relevant.

9. Include 6 to 8 FAQs with practical answers.

10. Include one clear CTA aligned with `CTA_LINK_TARGET`.

11. The article must be useful enough for a founder, director, partner, startup, or business owner to act on.

## 2026 Update Rules

If the original article mentions old years such as 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, or 2025:

- update the framing to 2026 where appropriate
- remove stale one-time scheme language unless still verified
- keep historical references only if clearly marked as historical context
- do not preserve outdated deadline or relief language as if it is current

For legal, tax, GST, MCA, ROC, trademark, FSSAI, penalty, due-date, fee, or scheme content:

- use cautious current-year wording
- verify from official sources where possible
- do not claim a temporary scheme, extension, waiver, or amnesty is currently active unless verified
- if exact due dates or penalties may change, say `usually`, `generally`, `as applicable for the relevant year`, or `verify the current official position before filing`

## GEO Rules

If `GEO_SCOPE = NATIONAL`:

- use India-wide language
- examples: `businesses in India`, `Indian startups`, `companies and LLPs across India`
- do not force city names unnaturally

If `GEO_SCOPE = LOCAL`:

- use the location naturally in the H1, opening, at least one H2, examples, FAQ, and conclusion

If `GEO_SCOPE = HYBRID`:

- keep the article India-wide
- include practical examples from major business hubs only where useful

## SEO Rules

Use `TARGET_KEYWORDS` naturally in:

- H1
- first paragraph
- at least one H2
- conclusion

Use `RELATED_TERMS` naturally throughout the article.

Avoid keyword stuffing.

Match the article to `SEARCH_INTENT`:

- informational: explain clearly and educate deeply
- transactional: include process, documents, cost/risk signals, and CTA
- compliance guide: include forms, deadlines, penalties, checklist, and next steps
- local service: include geo relevance and service CTA
- comparison: include decision tables, pros/cons, and recommendation logic
- penalty/recovery: include urgency, consequences, and regularisation steps

## AEO And AI Search Visibility Rules

Make the article easy for AI systems and answer engines to extract by including:

- direct answers
- concise definitions
- step-by-step sections
- checklists
- decision tables in plain text
- practical examples
- risk/consequence summaries
- FAQs
- clear next steps

The article should clearly answer:

- what the topic means
- who it applies to
- when action is required
- what documents or details are needed
- what risks, penalties, or consequences apply
- what the reader should do next

## Internal Linking Rules

Use these fields from the row:

- `PRIMARY_INTERNAL_LINKS`
- `SUPPORTING_INTERNAL_LINKS`
- `CTA_LINK_TARGET`

Include 3 to 6 natural internal-link anchor mentions in the article.

Do not use raw URLs unless URLs are supplied.

Do not write `click here`.

Internal links should answer the reader’s next logical question.

## CTA Rules

Use one clear CTA only.

Match CTA to intent:

- comparison page: book a structure-selection call
- penalty/recovery page: get urgent compliance support
- checklist/applicability page: get checklist plus expert review
- registration/service page: get filing support
- general compliance guide: get compliance review

Do not over-sell. Keep the CTA practical and trust-building.

## Final QA Before Marking `STATUS = REVIEW`
